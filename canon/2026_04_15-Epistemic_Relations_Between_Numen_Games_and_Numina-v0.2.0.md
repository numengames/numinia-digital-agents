# Epistemic Relations between Numen Games and Numinia

To begin with, it is important to clearly establish the principle of the relationship between Numen Games and Numinia: Numen Games is a brand, a project aimed at creating a gamified operating system permeated by narrative, whether as a specific framework or an organizational body within any type of project under development. To this end, Numen Games has created the universe of the city of Numinia, which constitutes the narrative projection of the concept. Numinia is the environment in which “the game” unfolds, forming the gamification of the operational and organizational framework as a role-playing game. The imagery implied by the narrative of Numinia is designed to function as a decentralized organizational model with epistemological and pragmatic values, which can be replicated with different narrative elements to apply the same regulatory model to other projects. This can be better understood with the following scheme:

## Scheme

GERMINAL MOTIVE → REGULATORY MODEL → NARRATIVE PROJECTION

In this scheme, the Germinal Motive represents any idea of an organizational project, whether a work framework or a social framework for other purposes, with specific and organizational needs. It takes the name “motive” from its morphology: it is an abstract idea driven by an order of intentions, constituting a figure that, even without development, already presents a recognizable semblance, both through its motivations and its initial sociocultural patterns.

For its part, the Regulatory Model is the structure that addresses these needs, with a complex and well-defined architecture of roles, positions, contexts, environments, tools, and concepts. It thus forms the skeleton on which the idea is sustained. As a structure, it is an invariant of a hypothetical nature; it emerges from the need for realization of the Germinal Motive, which manifests in the Narrative Projection. As the Narrative Projection develops, the figure of the Regulatory Model emerges, without determining whether its presence is original or arises a posteriori, with the action of development itself. In any case, from the moment it emerges and presents itself as a hypothesis, it becomes a reference for both the Germinal Motive and the Narrative Projection. And it is an invariant object because, although composed of a considerable number of variable and changing elements, and motivating a transformative action, it presents a hypothetical body that resists all transformation, remaining invariable. This character is indispensable to remain as an argumentative hypothesis, as a regulatory motive that validates the Germinal Motive and mediates the Narrative Projection.

Finally, the Narrative Projection is the literary and gamified representation of this model (although the very architecture of the Regulatory Model already points toward a gamified character), providing narrative aspects to all the elements of the complex structure that constitutes the model.

The sequence of this scheme responds to a merely conceptual and mental arrangement, based on necessity: the seminal idea of a project creates a series of purposes; these purposes require a structural model on which to be sustained; the model inevitably projects itself into a narrative in order to be understood, to become tangible. But this is a purely theoretical arrangement, since in practice, as already explained, the transition from one value to another follows a less defined progression. Generally, the idea arises from a spontaneous narrative, and this generates awareness of the structural model; or the original idea gives rise to a spontaneous narrative, which, in turn, once again causes the structural model to emerge. In any case, the sequence is mentally organized in the conceptual arrangement presented in the diagram, in which the model is the link between the seminal object and its narrative manifestation, in order to clarify the three elements, for a simple matter of logic.

In any case, it must be understood that, although they are three distinguishable elements, they are not dissociable. Together they form a Borromean knot, a unit composed of three interlinked rings among which there exists a relationship of deep interdependence; a unit in which it is not always easy to discern boundaries and in which the interaction and connection between the three elements is absolute. And its dynamics are more similar to a disordered movement among them.

Thus, the Operating System of Numen Games consists of the co-implication between a Germinal Motive as an abstract conceptual core, its Narrative Projection as a physical manifestation, and a Regulatory Structure that can be interpreted both as a prior condition (a priori form) and as emerging (a posteriori form), without it being necessary for this ambivalence to be definitively resolved in order for it to operate as a condition of possibility and constitute the structural reference of the project from the very moment of its emergence.

Numen Games seeks to build this Operating System on well-founded epistemological and ontological bases. To this end, principles of Systems Thinking and Active Inference are studied, as well as disciplines related to thought and logos, such as Semantics, Semiotics, Hermeneutics, Linguistics, Psychology, or Philosophy, for their application in the construction of the complex structure that is the model.

To consolidate this idea, we will turn to the analogous behavior of symbolic registers in a semiotic analysis, which helps us understand the relationship between the three elements. Charles Peirce’s semiotic model is ideal for this, since its tendency to structure semiotic analysis into trichotomies aligns with the Borromean knot of three elements that constitutes the principle of relationship between Numen Games and Numinia.

## Peircean Model

Peirce establishes the following elements for the sign: the object, the representamen, and the interpretant. If the representamen is the physical representation of the object, the object is the abstract concept toward which the representamen points. It is understood, then, that if the sign has an origin, it is the mental idea that constitutes the object, and it is its existence that projects the physical (visual or auditory) representation that is the representamen. In some way, we could speak of expression (representamen) and content (object), if that offers a clearer idea of the concepts, although it may be a reduction of them. The interpretant, for its part, is the idea of a new sign that the representamen provokes in the mind of the interpreter. It would then be a narrative formed in the psychic process of the interpreter at the moment they perceive the sign; a narrative that ends up constructing another sign. For Peirce, this process is infinite, with this new sign, when expressed, having a new representamen, which will provoke in another interpreter a new interpretant, and so on. However, Peirce does not settle for this trichotomy and introduces the idea of the ground. The ground is a specific position that the object occupies in the world of representations, and, in that sense, it can be understood as a referent. It is, for the object, a reference that applies a concrete meaning to it. Thus, Peirce forms a new trichotomy, in which ground, object, and interpretant are the three abstract elements related to the representamen, which is the physical element (a representation).

As will be seen, this fourth element, the ground, is essential in the model (which will no longer be formed by a triad, but by a tetrad—a tetrad that forms different triads, in any case). For this epistemic relationship to take on a pragmatic approach, we will take a complex sign that we can equate with the Borromean knot of the same relationship between Numinia and Numen Games. And since its intention is model-based, we will refer to the complex sign that constitutes the archetype.

We are interested in the Jungian symbolic characterization of the archetype (understanding the symbol as a form motivated by instinct, and not as a legisign or a sign in which the relationship between signified and signifier is arbitrary). According to Jung, the archetype is an unconscious and archaic mental schema that can only be brought to consciousness through myth. This bringing to consciousness is carried out through a mythologem, which comprises both the archetypal model and its representation. At this point, it is necessary to specify that, if the archetypal representation is a purely personal, subjective expression, it inevitably points toward an archetypal model, which is a collectively shared skeleton, since it constitutes a cultural unit that stems from an atavistic, universal concept inherent to human consciousness; it is, therefore, an intersubjective entity. It is precisely the archetypal model that enables the archetype to be brought to consciousness and recognized upon perceiving the archetypal representation (which, as a narrative, may be completely new to the interpreter). Moreover, it is through a process involving the three stages of aesthetic experience in Aristotelian terms (poiesis, aisthesis, and catharsis), in which the perception of the archetypal representation provokes a spontaneous narrative in the mind of the interpreter, allowing them to point toward the archetypal model and reach recognition (whether more or less conscious) from a completely new narrative for them.

All this helps us better understand Peirce’s semiotic model: the archetype, as an atavistic mental schema, is an abstract element situated at the origin of the idea, and would be, in Peirce’s model, the object; expressing it implies presenting an archetypal representation, which would be the representamen; but that representation can only be expressed because both the object (as origin) and the representamen (as term) point toward an archetypal model, which, as a referent, would be the ground, and would be situated between the origin and the term.

## Analogy with Numen Games

And now we can establish the analogy with the scheme that synthesizes the relationship between Numen Games and Numinia. The object, an abstract mental schema, would be the Germinal Motive; the ground, as referent, would be the Regulatory Model; and the representamen, the physical representation of the abstract mental schema, would be the Narrative Projection. We can think of the Borromean knot of the relationship between Numen Games and Numinia as an archetypal schema situated between atavistic mental frameworks and cultural units, but which adjusts to the sociocultural patterns of any intersubjective experience. For, finally, this Borromean knot provokes in the mind of the receiver a spontaneous narrative, the interpretant, which will form their own subjective (or intersubjective, whether observed in psychological or philosophical terms when that interpretation is reflected in a sociocultural framework) interpretation, creating a personal representation from the same model. That is, replicating the model in the development of another narrative projection. And so on.

And this explains the order followed by the scheme, from an origin idea, the Germinal Motive (vision of an internal representation, in pragmatic terms), to a terminal idea, the Narrative Projection (expression of an external representation), passing through a referent, the Regulatory Model, which provides a dimension of meaning to the scheme.

We can now specify that Numinia is the paradigmatic and prototypical example of this Narrative Projection, with a literary foundation that combines elements from the early 20th century (1900–1920) and the early 21st century (2000–2020), drawing an analogy between the social, scientific, and technological phenomena of one era and another. From this perspective, the literary creation of Numinia incorporates elements of science fiction and fantasy, as well as influences from cultures such as steampunk and cyberpunk. The intention is that this literary and dimensioned framework, as a Narrative Projection, covers the needs of the Regulatory and organizational Model, in the same way that the latter covers the needs of the Germinal Motive. Therefore, the literary framework must be meticulously aligned with all the elements of the complex system it encompasses.

And the purpose of this Narrative Projection of the Regulatory Model is for the Germinal Motive to become much more accessible and expansive through narrative and gameplay experience. Numen Games understands that if play is the basic principle of learning, and fiction (myth) constitutes the path to becoming aware of archetypal ideas, any executable task acquires dimension through the gameplay experience. Education, efficiency, and multiplicity sustain this Narrative Projection. Therefore, the function is not directly (or primarily) playful, but formative, educational, and operational; the fact that the gameplay experience is projected, by its nature, into a narrative experience more clearly constitutes its raison d’être and the teleological and axiological principles of its essentiality. Consciousness turns toward play, toward narrative, to learn, develop, expand, recognize itself, and realize itself.

## Connection with the Structure of Numinia

As seen, both Numen Games and Numinia constitute complex concepts that may be difficult to understand at first encounter. Anyone who has their first contact with Numen Games is already part of the Game, even without realizing it, just as any player who approaches Numinia with purely playful intentions will come into contact, even if unconsciously, with the epistemological and pragmatic foundations of Numen Games. Even those who are building the project are part of the Game. The gamification of the functional model is, therefore, a metagame, with Numen Games itself, as a brand, being an operating system that develops within a complex functional and organizational model with a dimensioned narrative projection called “Numinia.”

In this way, Numen Games understands that the best way to come into contact with this scheme (expressed in the previous section), as represented by Numinia, is invariably through the gameplay experience. Any initial approach and understanding must occur as a mythic-narrative exercise, and not through flat explanations that fail to reach the pragmatic dimension and further confuse potential user-player-participants, diminishing their interest.

As an imaginary city that exists in another dimension, Numinia develops in the metaverse. Different virtual spaces make up its tangible body.

## Connection with the Numen Games Framework

In principle, since Numen Games is itself a framework under a gamified Operating System and is enveloped by the imaginary of Numinia, connecting with Numen Games implies connecting with Numinia. However, there are certain particularities that develop on the Numen Games layer and are essential for interacting within its framework:

The main work meetings in Numen Games revolve around two events: Dark Council and Lunar Coven.

### Dark Council

Focused on tasks related to project development.

### Lunar Coven

Dedicated to gameplay and generally organize events within a space that constitutes an adventure (such as an escape room).

The Dark Councils take place on Mondays from 10:00 to 12:00 (UTC+1) at the Ateneo of Numinia. The Lunar Covens are held on Thursdays from 10:00 to 12:00 (UTC+1) in different spaces depending on that week’s adventure.
