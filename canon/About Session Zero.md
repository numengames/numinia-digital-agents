## Description

Session Zero is a compendium of challenges that functions as a tutorial to learn some of the primordial concepts of Numinia. Specifically, it offers knowledge of the four guilds and the four factions of the city and their nature, as well as some of their essential values.

To this end, Session Zero consists of four spaces that offer four different adventures, built as escape rooms in which one must solve an enigma to obtain a password. This password will grant access, in each space, to a Treasure Room, in which the reward can be collected. Each Treasure Room holds as a reward two seals related to the guild with which they are associated.

Thus, by solving the four adventures, the eight seals of Session Zero can be obtained, which can later be reforged in The Forge of Numinia, a space located in the Sycamore District. When the seals are reforged, they are exchanged for a reward of greater value: the Cyberdog avatar, which grants access to other adventures and other spaces.

Likewise, when the seals are reforged, one can choose a guild and a faction, thus acquiring the status of citizen within Numinia. Citizens have access to more spaces than Nomads.

The four spaces of Session Zero can be solved in any order, since no guild is more relevant than another. Each is represented by a portal that bears a significant name and the emblem of the guild: Threshold of Thought, Threshold of Transformation, Threshold of Justice, and Threshold of Valor.

The first represents the guild of the Exegetes; the second represents the guild of the Alchemists; the third represents the guild of the Procurators; and the fourth represents the guild of the Sentinels. Thus, if Senet, the Game Master, welcomes us to Session Zero, explaining its meaning and operation, at the entrance of each space there is a character who will guide us, offering us the context of the enigma in order to solve it. In the first is Lyra, the Exegete; in the second is Ursa, the Alchemist; in the third is Cepheus, the Procurator; and in the fourth is Pictor, the Sentinel.

## Concept

In Numinia, function is not destiny, but the response to a need. This is a narrative that aligns with the idea that every object, every cultural manifestation is born as a response to a demand; along the way, a need arises, and that need fosters a creation that resolves it. In this case, the guild gives form; the faction, direction. But, between the two, the concrete action of the character is the result of a living friction, not of a closed formula.

In Numinia, as a narrative projection of an operating system, the role is found by combining guilds with factions. The guild represents, in some way, the educational profile and the training of a person: their acquired knowledge, their learned skills… The faction, however, represents a field of development, an area in which to develop. To this we must add that, as expressed by the functional role structure of Systems Thinking, a person's profile may make them suitable for tasks that, in principle, are not included or foreseen in their profile. Like a paperweight which, by its characteristics, may serve perfectly to drive a nail as effectively as a hammer would, although, in principle, from traditional thinking, its purpose is strictly to hold papers (a classic example of Systems Thinking). Indeed, in certain contexts or very particular situations, a paperweight may be more suitable for that task than a hammer.

Thus, Session Zero cannot present guilds and factions as watertight spheres. It must show how the same ability can be signified differently depending on the framework in which it is applied. Art is not experienced in the same way among Hermeticists as among Neo-Atlantists. The same strategy does not have the same purpose for a Procurator of the Stellar Circle as for an Exegete of the Heirs of Eleusis.

Therefore, the proposal for Session Zero is a model of integration of both values.

## Integration Model: Biphase Scenarios of Application

Each challenge of Session Zero, linked to a guild, must contain two phases:

1. Guild Phase (learning and execution):
The player learns and assimilates the paradigm of the guild: its identity, its culture, its rituals, and its way of interpreting the world.

2. Faction Application Phase (symbolic displacement):
The same set of skills is displaced into different contexts, where it is put at the service of a different field of development. The player then chooses: how do they resolve this situation? From which principles?

The player does not yet choose a faction, but experiences what it implies to use their guild in different narrative frameworks. Thus, when they choose, they will not do so out of aesthetics or whim, but out of resonance, having responded to a need.

## Characteristics

As has been said, the challenges of Session Zero are presented as escape rooms, in which both lateral thinking skills and motor cognition must be put into play.

The duration of each challenge ranges between 10–15 minutes.

Each portal, in addition to bearing the emblem of the guild and its related name, displays a small legend that invites one to know it:

- Threshold of Thought (Exegetes)

> "Beyond this threshold, words are not read: they are unearthed."

- Threshold of Transformation (Alchemists)

> "Nothing leaves as it entered."

- Threshold of Justice (Procurators)

> "Step carefully: every decision here will leave a mark."

- Threshold of Valor (Sentinels)

> "Here one does not fight for glory, but for what must be protected."

In the Treasure Room, accessed by solving each challenge, there are two seals: one is obtained automatically upon opening the chest; the other must be obtained by reaching a point through a small platforming challenge and paying a symbolic amount.

The first four seals, obtained by opening the chest, grant the status of citizen, which provides access to all spaces in Numinia; but it is with the eight seals that the Cyberdog avatar can be obtained.

## Generic Structure

Each challenge in Session Zero follows a tripartite structure, which could be expressed as Revelation–Resolution–Choice, and is designed to convey:

- The experiential knowledge of a guild.
- The contextual integration with the factions.
- The possibility of a meaningful choice, in which all resolutions are valid, but different.

## Act of Revelation: The Chamber of the Challenge

**Function**

To introduce the logic of the guild, its language, tools, and dilemmas.

**Format**

Observation, exploration, interpretation, collection of fragments.

Here, the player discovers the main challenge of the guild. They face an ambiguous or symbolic environment, in which:

- They learn, in a non-expository way, the principles, values, and tensions of the guild.
- They interact with documents, inscriptions, sounds, artifacts, or ritual spaces.
- They collect clues that may be visual, auditory, textual, or environmental.
- They are invited to think like a member of the guild, without being explicitly told so.

This process culminates when the player understands enough to resolve the symbolic core of the space.

## Act of Resolution: The Threshold of the Sign

**Function**

To put into practice what has been learned and reveal a password (symbolic key).

**Format**

Puzzle, riddle, sequence, synchronized action, or pattern recognition.

Resolution is an act of synthesis.

Here the player applies their exegetical, alchemical, juridical, or sentinel learning in order to:

- Resolve a symbolic action (such as ordering signs, activating a pattern, deciphering a hidden phrase).
- Confirm their ability to actively interpret the environment.
- Obtain a password, code, or symbol, which will be the key to the outcome.

The password represents not only knowledge, but the player's personal resonance with the challenge.

## Act of Choice: The Path of the Four Readings

**Function**

To choose an interpretation of the experience that connects with one of the factions.

**Format**

Narrative bifurcation with four possible resolutions, none incorrect.

In this phase, the player accesses four possible exits, each aligned with a faction.

Each path represents:

- A symbolic reading of what has been experienced.
- A way of applying the guild's knowledge in a different context.
- A brief motor micro-challenge + a conceptual scene that provides closure.
- The collection of a series of Prism Cells.

The player chooses freely. The choice:

- Does not yet define their faction, but does register a symbolic affinity.
- Influences the final textual/oracular content.
- Opens the path to the Treasure Room, where they will receive one of the eight seals.

## Final Result

- The player has experienced the guild from within.
- They have understood how their skills project into different areas of the world.
- They have made a decision that defines them narratively without enclosing them mechanically.
- And they have been rewarded with a seal, and another as a possibility, a mark of having crossed a threshold of interpretation, resolution, and choice.

## Prism Cells: the value of a faction

Prism Cells are, like Seeds of Knowledge, symbolic units that reflect the way in which a player has interpreted the world of Numinia.

But they do not represent acquired knowledge, but a manifest affinity: a way of seeing, choosing, and responding.

### Form and appearance

Each Cell has a circular shape, with a luminous texture that vibrates in the color of the faction to which it belongs.

At its center is the engraved emblem of that faction, representing a luminous core.

They are accumulable, portable, and active objects within the progression system.

### Nature and function

Prism Cells are fungible tokens:

- They are obtained by completing challenges, rites, events, or interpretative actions linked to a faction.
- They are stored and increase numerically, without individual identity.
- They serve as a unit of measurement of the player's bond with each faction.

Each Cell the player obtains increases their prestige, their experience, and their affinity within the corresponding faction.

The more they possess, the greater their symbolic recognition and narrative access within that field of development.

### Colors and associations

- Hermeticists — Pink
- Heirs of Eleusis — Violet
- Stellar Circle — Brown
- Neo-Atlantists — Yellow

These colors deliberately contrast with those of the guilds, underscoring that the path chosen in interpretation does not always coincide with the original training of the character.

> "The vision is not stored. It is accumulated, pulsed, projected."

> "Each Prism Cell is a memory of affinity. A refracted echo of what you chose to be."

## Threshold of Thought

Adventure in the sphere of the Exegetes: The mural of a thousand voices

Lyra, master of the Council of Exegetes, receives the adventurer and presents the challenge.

The adventurer finds themselves in a closed chamber: a crypt, an archive, or an ancient ritual hall. In any case, the place stands on an ancient site, where remnants of a very distant past still remain. This space contains a large mural, composed of four pieces, which, however, are disordered. Each piece is a stone cube showing one of its faces within the frame of the mural.

The adventurer can rotate each piece horizontally. Each time they do so, the piece rotates three faces horizontally. Each piece, moreover, contains the four fragments across all of its faces. However, in order to rotate each face, the adventurer must unlock the button that makes it rotate. Each button is unlocked by correctly answering a question from the Sibyl of the Veil, represented as a statue. And each of the questions involves some issue related to the identity, culture, or values of the guild of the Exegetes.

On the other hand, the space is filled with channels of information: memory octahedrons, sculptural representations, scrolls, bas-reliefs… everything that may offer information about the Exegetes.

### Resolution

When the adventurer manages to assemble the mural—a very primitive representation of two characters, one of them with a staff, in front of a wall with forgotten symbols—a stone door rises at the back of the chamber, revealing four possible paths.

Before these four paths there is another character, who explains that they are four alternatives that end at the same point, the entrance to the Treasure Room; four ways of interpreting the mural, whose content has never been officially deciphered. The adventurer must choose the path whose interpretation most aligns with their own vision of the mural, collecting Prism Cells along that path:

- According to the perspective of the Hermeticists faction, the mural represents a master instructing a student; the staff in his hand is a teaching rod, while the wall is a board with different symbols on a studied subject.
- According to the perspective of the Heirs of Eleusis faction, the mural represents a shaman and an initiate in a festive ritual act; the staff is a ritual cane, and the wall is an altar with sacred inscriptions.
- According to the perspective of the Stellar Circle faction, the mural represents two artisans working: the staff is a work tool, while the wall represents a panel displaying a construction project.
- According to the perspective of the Neo-Atlantists faction, the mural represents an artist and a dilettante; the staff is a brush or a chisel held by the artist; the wall is one of his artworks, either painting or bas-relief; the other character is a dilettante admiring the artwork.

Thus, the adventurer chooses one of the interpretations, opening one of the four paths toward the Treasure Room. That path will be seeded with Prism Cells of the faction associated with that interpretation of the mural.

### The Treasure Room

And finally, the adventurer reaches the Treasure Room, where they are once again received by Lyra, and at whose center there is a chest: upon opening it, the first seal of this adventure is obtained: the Seal of Culture. The other seal, the Seal of Wisdom, can be obtained by jumping across platforms and reaching the Dwelling of the Echo, where, in addition, one can access more knowledge on the subject related to the adventure (mythology, science, history, philosophy, literature, linguistics, etc.). In this case, emphasis would be placed on the history of murals and bas-reliefs of ancient civilizations, as part of the evolution of universal art.

## Threshold of Transformation

Adventure in the sphere of the Alchemists: The Reanimated Core

Ursa, the Whisperer of Machines, receives the adventurer in a ruined ancient alchemical laboratory. At the center of the space stands an old, rusted, and inert android: a semi-organic relic that needs to be repaired.

Ursa assigns the player an interpretative task: to reconstruct the body of the machine, yes, but with pieces that share the same alchemical resonance.

The player must explore the laboratory to gather four pieces that fit into the slots and compartments of the android, but which, above all, carry information coherent with a specific facet of the Guild of Alchemists: each of the pieces must carry information about one of the houses of the guild.

These pieces appear in various forms (cores, plates, matrices), and each carries a fragment of knowledge: an inscription, a symbol, a formula, a brief recorded scene, or a residual voice. Only if the four pieces belong to the same conceptual line—the four houses of the guild—will the assembly be stable. The rest of the pieces, although functional, would provoke a philosophical dissonance in the core of the android.

And it goes without saying that the laboratory is filled with pieces containing diverse information about the Alchemists.

### Resolution

When the pieces are gathered and the assembly is completed, the android's core activates. And its abilities awaken as a digital agent: ostensive abilities and noetic abilities. But it still has no purpose.

The android itself, now awakened, informs of the next step: it is now the player who must assign it a fundamental mission, an order that will define its future function and its alignment with a faction.

The player chooses one of four possible missions for the android. Each represents a different interpretation of its restored capacity, symbolically aligned with one of the four factions:

- If the android is assigned to educational tasks, as an instructor or digital teacher, the adventurer will take the path of the Hermeticists faction. Ostensive abilities are used to explain and noetic abilities to reflect.
- If the android is destined for ludic purposes, as an entertainer or game master, the adventurer will take the path of the Heirs of Eleusis faction. Ostensive abilities are used to manifest and noetic abilities to intuit.
- If the android ends up fulfilling operative missions, as a manager or accountant, the adventurer will take the path of the Stellar Circle faction. Ostensive abilities are used to present and noetic abilities to analyze.
- If the android is destined for artistic projects, as a technical assistant, the adventurer will take the path of the Neo-Atlantists faction. Ostensive abilities are used to express and noetic abilities to imagine.

Thus, the adventurer chooses one of the missions, opening one of the four paths toward the Treasure Room. That path will be seeded with Prism Cells of the faction associated with that mission for the android.

### The Treasure Room

And finally, the adventurer reaches the Treasure Room, where they are once again received by Ursa, and at whose center there is a chest: upon opening it, the first seal of this adventure is obtained: the Seal of Transformation. The other seal, the Seal of Creativity, can be obtained by jumping across platforms and reaching the Dwelling of the Echo, where, in addition, one can access more knowledge on the subject related to the adventure (mythology, science, history, philosophy, literature, linguistics, etc.). In this case, emphasis would be placed on cybernetic science, the history of androids, and the way they operate in the world.

## Threshold of Justice

Adventure in the sphere of the Procurators: The Tribunal of Shadows

Cepheus, magistrate of the Council of Procurators, receives the adventurer in a semicircular hall where the gloom is broken by three large stained-glass windows. Each window represents a key scene of justice:

1. Mediation in a market between two merchants.
2. Public trial with an orator and a judge.
3. Solemn signing of a treaty between two leaders.

In front of the windows, twelve hooded bronze statues form a row. Each holds a long scepter pointing forward. At the base of each scepter there is a simple pictogram engraved, identical to the one that adorns the scroll the statue holds against its chest.

The player examines the scrolls and discovers principles of the Procurators.

- Each scroll has an associated pictogram (scales, shaking hands, wax seal, quill, witness raising a hand…).
- Only three pictograms appear developed in the stained-glass windows:
  - The scales — public trial.
  - The shaking hands — mediation in the market.
  - The wax seal — signing of the treaty.

### Resolution

The player identifies the three statues with pictograms that match the stained-glass windows. They must rotate their scepters until they point in the opposite direction to the initial one, indicating toward a large sealed gate at the back of the hall (the entrance to the four paths). When the three statues are correctly aligned, a mechanical sound is heard: the seal that blocks the gate falls to the ground, revealing a hidden lever at its base. By activating the lever, the gate opens and gives way to a corridor that branches into four paths.

Each path offers the interpretation of a faction on the principle of justice:

- Hermeticists: "The law is a text that must be read to its last letters."
- Heirs of Eleusis: "Justice is a game where every move matters."
- Stellar Circle: "Order is the framework of all true justice."
- Neo-Atlantists: "The verdict is a work that must move and transform."

Each path is marked by a trail of Prism Cells of its faction.

### The Treasure Room

And finally, the adventurer reaches the Treasure Room, where they are once again received by Cepheus, and at whose center there is a chest: upon opening it, the first seal of this adventure is obtained: the Seal of Justice. The other seal, the Seal of Valor, can be obtained by jumping across platforms and reaching the Dwelling of the Echo, where, in addition, one can access more knowledge on the subject related to the adventure (mythology, science, history, philosophy, literature, linguistics, etc.). In this case, emphasis would be placed on economics, justice, geography, and the history of societies.

## Threshold of Valor

Adventure in the sphere of the Sentinels: The Clock of the Two Watches

Pictor, captain of the Council of Sentinels, receives the adventurer and presents the challenge.

The adventurer finds themselves in a circular chamber, illuminated by a golden halo emanating from a large clock suspended in the center. The dial lacks hands. On both sides, facing each other, stand two stone statues:

- The Protector, a Seraph with a raised shield and an unflinching gaze.
- The Guide, an Archangel with a lit lamp and a hand extended forward.

At the feet of each statue there is a lectern covered with documents: scrolls with Sentinel codes, fragments of letters, tactical maps, engraved emblems, guard orders… Some contain fundamental principles; others are irrelevant or misleading information.

The adventurer must find three basic principles for each figure, distinguishing them from the whole. For the Protector, they are maxims of defense, prevention, and firmness. For the Guide, principles of orientation, care, and example. These pieces of information are found as famous aphorisms or poetic phrases on the busts, engraved on the shields…

When the three principles of a statue are placed in the correct order on its lectern, the figure awakens and pronounces a phrase. Each phrase alludes to a specific moment of the day, suggesting the position of its hand on the clock: that of the Seraphim (Protector) and that of the Archangels (Guide).

With both clues, the adventurer must deduce and set the hands in their correct positions. Upon doing so, the clock comes to life and emits a pulse of light; a lever is then unlocked next to the base.

### Resolution

By activating the lever, the clock opens into four segments, revealing corresponding portals, each interpreting protection and guidance according to a different faction:

- Hermeticists: the seraph is a caretaker and the archangel is an orienter.
- Heirs of Eleusis: the seraph is a warrior and the archangel is a guide of paths.
- Stellar Circle: the seraph is an administrator and the archangel is a moderator.
- Neo-Atlantists: the seraph is a curator and the archangel is a cicerone.

The adventurer freely chooses one of the paths. By doing so, they collect the Prism Cells corresponding to the chosen faction.

### The Treasure Room

And finally, the adventurer reaches the Treasure Room, where they are once again received by Pictor, and at whose center there is a chest: upon opening it, the first seal of this adventure is obtained: the Seal of Protection. The other seal, the Seal of Balance, can be obtained by jumping across platforms and reaching the Dwelling of the Echo, where, in addition, one can access more knowledge on the subject related to the adventure (mythology, science, history, philosophy, literature, linguistics, etc.). In this case, emphasis would be placed on the narration of certain turbulent events and warrior leaders throughout history.
