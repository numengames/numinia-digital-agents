# Epistemic Relations between Numen Games and Numinia

> **Resumen:** Documento del sistema NWOS — Epistemic relations between Numen Games and Numinia.
> **Epistémico:** Qué aprendes leyendo este documento.
> **Pragmático:** Qué puedes hacer con este documento.
> **Audiencia:** Agentes · Oráculos

---


To begin with, it is important to clearly establish the principle of the relationship between Numen Games and Numinia: Numen Games is a brand, a project intended to create a gamified operational and organizational framework, whether as a specific working framework or an organizational framework within any type of project or development. To achieve this, Numen Games has created the universe of the city of Numinia, which constitutes the narrative projection of the concept. Numinia is the environment in which "the game" unfolds, forming the gamification of the operational and organizational framework as a role-playing game.

The images implied by the narrative of Numinia are designed to function as a decentralized organizational model with epistemological and pragmatic values, which can be replicated with different narrative elements to apply the same functional model to other projects. This can be better understood with the following scheme:

> **OPERATING SYSTEM → FUNCTIONAL MODEL → NARRATIVE PROJECTION**

In this scheme, the Operating System represents any idea of an organizational project, whether a working framework or a social framework for other purposes, with specific and organizational needs. For its part, the Functional Model is the structure that addresses these needs, with a complex and well-defined architecture of roles, positions, contexts, environments, tools, and concepts. It is, then, the skeleton upon which the idea is sustained. Finally, the Narrative Projection is the literary and gamified representation of this model (although the very architecture of the Functional Model already has a gamification character), providing narrative aspects to all the elements of the complex structure that constitutes the model.

The sequence of this scheme responds to a mere conceptual arrangement, based on necessity: the seminal idea of a project creates a series of purposes; these purposes require a structural model upon which to be sustained; the model inevitably projects itself into a narrative in order to be understood, to become tangible.

In practice, the sequence is more disordered (generally, the idea arises from a spontaneous narrative and this generates awareness of the structural model), but little by little it is organized into this conceptual arrangement, in which the model is the link between the organizational object and its narrative manifestation, in order to clarify the three elements. However, it must be understood that, although they are distinguishable, they are not separable. The three elements form a unity in which it is not always easy to discern the boundaries and in which the interaction and connection between them are absolute. And their dynamics are more akin to a circular movement among the three elements.

Numen Games seeks to build this functional model on well-founded epistemological and ontological bases. To this end, principles of Systems Thinking and Active Inference are studied, as well as disciplines related to thought and logos, such as Semantics, Semiotics, Hermeneutics, Linguistics, Psychology, or Philosophy, for their application in the construction of the complex structure that is the model.

To consolidate this idea, we will resort to the analogous behavior of symbolic registers in a semiotic analysis, which helps us understand the relationship between the three elements. Charles Peirce's semiotic model is ideal for this, as its tendency to structure semiotic analysis in trichotomies fits the Borromean knot of three elements that forms the principle of the relationship between Numen Games and Numinia.

Peirce establishes the following elements for the sign: the object, the representamen, and the interpretant. If the representamen is the representation of the object, the object is that toward which the representamen points. It is understood, then, that if the sign has an origin, it is the abstract and mental idea that constitutes the object, and it is its existence that projects the physical (visual or auditory) representation that is the representamen. In some way, we could speak of expression (representamen) and content (object), if this offers a clearer idea of the concepts, although it is a reduction of them.

The interpretant, for its part, is the idea of a new sign that the representamen provokes in the mind of the interpreter. It would then be a narrative formed in the interpreter's psychic process at the moment they perceive the sign; a narrative that ends up constructing another sign. For Peirce, this process is infinite, with this new sign, when expressed, having a new representamen, which will provoke in another interpreter a new interpretant, and so on.

However, Peirce does not settle for this trichotomy and introduces the idea of ground. The ground is a specific position that the object occupies in the world of representations, and in that sense it can be understood as a referent. It is, for the object, a reference that applies a concrete meaning to it. Thus, Peirce forms a new trichotomy, in which ground, object, and interpretant are the three abstract elements that relate to the representamen, which is the physical element (a representation).

As will be seen, this fourth element, the ground, is essential in the model (which will no longer be formed by a triad, but by a tetrad (a tetrad that forms different triads, in any case)). For this epistemic relationship to take on a pragmatic focus, we will take a complex sign that we can equate to the Borromean knot of the same relationship between Numinia and Numen Games. And since its intention is model-like, we will refer to the complex sign that constitutes the archetype.

We are interested in the Jungian symbolic characterization of the archetype (understanding the symbol as a form motivated by instinct, and not as a legisign). According to Jung, the archetype is an unconscious and archaic mental scheme that can only be made conscious through myth. This conscientization is carried out through a mythologem, which comprises both the archetypal model and its representation.

At this point, it is necessary to specify that, if the archetypal representation is a purely personal, subjective expression, it inevitably points toward an archetypal model, which is a collectively shared skeleton (since it constitutes a cultural unity that stems from an atavistic, universal concept, inherent to human consciousness). It is precisely the archetypal model that facilitates the archetype being made conscious and recognized upon perceiving the archetypal representation (which, as a narrative, may be completely new to the interpreter).

Moreover, it is through a process that involves the three stages of aesthetic experience in Aristotelian terms (poiesis, aisthesis, and catharsis), in which the perception of the archetypal representation provokes a spontaneous narrative in the interpreter's mind, allowing them to point toward the archetypal model and arrive at recognition (whether more or less conscious) from a completely new narrative.

All of this helps us better understand Peirce's semiotic model: the archetype, as an atavistic mental scheme, is an abstract element situated at the origin of the idea, and would be, in Peirce's model, the object; expressing it involves presenting an archetypal representation, which would be the representamen; but that representation can only be expressed because both the object (as origin) and the representamen (as term) point toward an archetypal model, which, as a referent, would be the ground, and would be situated between the origin and the term.

And now we can establish the analogy with the scheme that synthesizes the relationship between Numen Games and Numinia. The object, an abstract mental scheme, would be the Operating System; the ground, as referent, would be the Functional Model; and the representamen, the physical representation of the abstract mental scheme, would be the Narrative Projection.

We can think of the Borromean knot of the relationship between Numen Games and Numinia as an archetypal scheme situated between atavistic mental frameworks and cultural units, but which adapts to the sociocultural patterns of any subjective experience. For, finally, this Borromean knot provokes in the mind of the receiver a spontaneous narrative, the interpretant, which will shape their own subjective (or intersubjective, whether observed in psychological or philosophical terms when that interpretation is reflected in a sociocultural framework) interpretation, creating a personal representation based on the same model. That is, replicating the model in the development of another narrative projection. And so on.

And this explains the order followed by the scheme, from an idea of origin, the Operating System (vision of an internal representation, in pragmatic terms), to an idea of term, the Narrative Projection (expression of an external representation), passing through a referent, the Functional Model, which provides a dimension of meaning to the scheme.

We can now specify that Numinia is the paradigmatic and prototypical example of this narrative projection, with a literary base that combines elements from the early 20th century (1900–1920) and the early 21st century (2000–2020), drawing an analogy between the social, scientific, and technological phenomena of one era and another. From this perspective, the literary creation of Numinia incorporates elements of science fiction and fantasy, as well as influences from cultures such as steampunk and cyberpunk. The intention is for this literary and dimensional framework, as a narrative projection, to cover the needs of the functional and organizational model, in the same way that the latter covers the needs of the operating system. Therefore, the literary framework must align meticulously with all the elements of the complex structure it encompasses.

And the purpose of this Narrative Projection of the Functional Model is for the Operating System to become much more accessible and expansive through narrative and gameplay experience. Numen Games understands that, if play is the basic principle of learning, and fiction (myth) constitutes the path to becoming aware of archetypal ideas, any executable task acquires dimension through the experience of play.

Education, efficiency, and multiplicity underpin this narrative projection. Therefore, the function is not directly (or primarily) ludic, but formative, educational, and operational; the fact that the gameplay experience is projected, by its nature, into a narrative experience more clearly constitutes its raison d'être and the teleological and axiological principles of its essentiality. Consciousness turns toward play, toward narrative, in order to learn, develop, gain dimension, recognize itself, and realize itself.

## Connection with the Structure of Numinia

As has been seen, both Numen Games and Numinia constitute complex concepts that may be difficult to understand at first encounter. Anyone who has their first contact with Numen Games is already part of the Game, even without realizing it, just as any player who approaches Numinia with purely ludic intentions will come into contact, even if unconsciously, with the epistemological and pragmatic foundations of Numen Games. Even those who are building the project are part of the Game.

The gamification of the functional model is, therefore, a metagame, with Numen Games itself, as a brand, being an operating system that develops within a complex functional and organizational model with a dimensional narrative projection called "Numinia."

In this way, Numen Games understands that the best way to come into contact with this scheme (expressed in the previous section), as represented by Numinia, is invariably through the experience of play. Any initial approach and understanding must occur as a mythic-narrative exercise, and not through flat explanations that fail to reach the pragmatic dimension and further confuse potential users-players-participants.

As an imaginary city that exists in another dimension, Numinia unfolds in the metaverse. Different virtual spaces compose its tangible body.

## Connection with the Numen Games Framework

In principle, since Numen Games is itself a framework under a gamified operational model and is enveloped by the imaginary of Numinia, connecting with Numen Games implies connecting with Numinia. However, there are certain particularities that develop on the Numen Games layer and are essential for interacting within its framework:

The main working meetings in Numen Games revolve around two events: Dark Council and Lunar Coven.

- **Dark Council:** Focused on tasks related to project development.
- **Lunar Coven:** Dedicated to gameplay and generally organize events within a space that constitutes an adventure (such as an escape room).

Dark Councils take place on Mondays from 10:00 to 12:00 (UTC+1) at the Athenaeum of Numinia. Lunar Covens are held on Thursdays from 10:00 to 12:00 (UTC+1) in different spaces depending on that week's adventure.
