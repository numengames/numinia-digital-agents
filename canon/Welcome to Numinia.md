# Introduction to the Gamified System

Numinia is the narrative heart of the organizational model of Numen Games. It is not just a fantasy city: it is a living and active representation of the gamified operational environment that transforms real projects into meaningful adventures.

This model is based on the idea that any organizational system can gain dimension and clarity if projected as a game. Here, work and collaboration structures are translated into playful experiences: tasks become missions, roles become characters, and goals become shared challenges.

## What Does It Mean to Gamify Operativity?

To gamify is not to "play" without purpose. In Numinia, gamification means transforming operational dynamics into functional narratives, where every element of the system—be it a team, a task, a tool, or a person—plays a role within a coherent narrative universe.

This allows for:

- Increasing the understanding of complex structures by narrating them as scenarios and adventures.
- Strengthening motivation and participation by turning routine into exploration.
- Generating a replicable model, where any project can adopt this playful framework to give dimension to its work and make it more human, creative, and sustainable.

Numinia is projected from an "operating system" as if it were a living city: decisions are made in mythical assemblies, resources are managed in alchemical towers, and human relationships are cultivated in taverns and academies. Here, every role has meaning, and every action a tangible consequence.

That is why we say that in Numinia, to play is to operate, and to operate is to play. This is the space where work gains purpose through myth, organization becomes art, and every citizen is the protagonist of a collective story.

# Epistemic Relationship Between Numen Games and Numinia

To understand the ecosystem of Numinia, it is essential to grasp the deep relationship it maintains with Numen Games. These two entities are not separate things, but different expressions of the same idea: an organizational environment brought to life through play and narrative.

This relationship can be explained on three interconnected levels:

1. **Numen Games**
   It is the operating system: the set of ideas, principles, and organizational needs that give rise to the model. It represents the seed idea from which everything is built.
   → In Peirce's semiotic model, this is the Object.

2. **Functional Model**
   It is the organizational structure that responds to the needs of the operating system: a network of roles, spaces, tools, and concepts with pragmatic value.
   → This is where the Ground is located, which gives meaning to the object by defining how it should work.

3. **Numinia**
   It is the narrative projection of the functional model. A fantastical city that turns abstract structures into living and understandable experiences, where organizational functioning is narrated and played.
   → It is the Representamen, the visible expression of the system.

This structure is not linear, but cyclical: what is narrated affects the model, the model transforms the system, and the system gives rise to new narratives. Thus, to play in Numinia is to interpret, operate, and build the reality of the project.

# The Guilds

The guilds represent the knowledge profiles and areas of specialization of the citizens. Each one is structured hierarchically in three levels: Guild (general level), Branch (basic level), and House (specific level). This structure follows the semantic logic of the Basic Level Theory, where branches represent the most descriptive level. However, the level that proves most efficient for reflecting and recognizing missions is the general one—the Guild—which is used for the broadest and most global operational actions.

The four guilds are:

- Alchemists: Imagination, invention, and creation.
- Exegetes: History, theory, and narrative.
- Procurators: Management, law, and organization.
- Sentinels: Care, moderation, and guidance.

---

# The Factions

The factions group together domains of development and purpose. They are arranged in a horizontal logic, following the Prototype Theory of semantics, where Gamification acts as the central element, being the intermediate and inevitable step between Education and Organization; meanwhile, Art is an itinerant element, which can even be present in any of the other three elements.

Factions do not define skills, but rather visions and paths within the universe:

- Education: Research and applied knowledge.
- Gamification: Fantasy and fictional narrative.
- Organization: Structural and methodological framework.
- Art: Creativity and symbolic expression.

# The Role in Numinia

In Numinia, the Role is not a fixed label nor a description of skills. It is a functional identity that arises contextually and pragmatically. It is based on the Systems Thinking model, which understands that roles are not defined by people's static characteristics, but by the missions they assume at a given moment.

## Functional Perspective on the Role

Just as a paperweight, designed to hold down sheets, can perfectly be used to hammer a nail if the occasion requires it—since it has the qualities to perform that task—a person in Numinia can assume different roles depending on the tasks, challenges, or contexts that arise, even if they do not seem to fit their typical profile.

For example:

- An actor does not define their role based on their qualities, but by the character they play in a specific theatrical piece.

## Need and Action

In Numinia, the role is activated when someone takes on a task, which responds to a need. That function does not need to be pre-assigned, nor is it permanent. It is chosen or emerges based on three things:

1. The person's profile (their knowledge, skills, and attributes).
2. The need of the moment.
3. The context in which the action takes place.

This view promotes flexibility, collaboration, and a sense of shared purpose. Because here, to do is to be.

# Missions: Contributing to the Operating System

In Numinia, the concrete actions that help build, sustain, or improve the ecosystem are called missions. A mission can be a one-time task, an ongoing project, or a long-term collaboration. What defines a mission is not its size, but its purpose within the Numen Games operating system.

## A Living Board for Living Missions

All missions are organized and managed from a shared board where the community can:

- See what tasks are available.
- Propose new ones.
- Provide context, describe objectives, or tag them to facilitate understanding.
- Track their evolution in real time.

Currently, the board used in Numinia is Huli, an agile and visual tool that allows users to:

- Create new missions from scratch.
- Assign tasks to specific members or leave them open.
- Add clear descriptions and thematic tags, as well as sub-missions.
- Organize the workflow in columns: To Do → In Progress → In Review → Done

This system fosters transparency, decentralized collaboration, and autonomy: every citizen can see what the community is working on and where they can best contribute their energy.

In short, missions are the way each person activates their role and gives meaning to their journey through Numinia.

# Numinia's Rituals: The Collective Pulse

Life in Numinia is interwoven with collective rituals that set the weekly rhythm of work, creation, and collaboration. These instances are not mere meetings: they are ritualized spaces of synchronization and meaning, where individual purposes align with those of the ecosystem.

## Daily: The Rhythm of the Day

📅 When: Every morning (except Tuesday and Friday)
🕘 Time: 09:00 (UTC+1)
⏳ Estimated Duration: 15 minutes – ½ hour

Each day begins with this short and focused meeting, in which each member:

- Shares what they worked on the day before.
- States what they will focus on that day.

It is skipped on Tuesdays and Fridays, as those days follow longer nightly rituals (Dark Council and Lunar Coven).

## Dark Council: The Strategy Assembly

📅 When: Every Monday
🕙 Time: 22:00 (UTC+1)
⏳ Estimated Duration: 1 hour (between 30 minutes and 2 hours)

This is the weekly strategic meeting, where the group reviews:

- What was accomplished during the previous week.
- Each member's purpose and focus for the upcoming week.
- The collective priorities and goals of the community.

It is a space to align visions, share concerns, and fine-tune direction.

## Lunar Coven: The Creative Circle

📅 When: Every Thursday
🕙 Time: 22:00 (UTC+1)
⏳ Estimated Duration: 1 hour (up to 2 hours)

With a more open and playful tone, this meeting is dedicated to:

- Testing games, mechanics, and prototypes.
- Exploring creative ideas.
- Discussing topics related to Numinia's playful and cultural experience.

The Lunar Coven is the ritual laboratory where the magic of the game is nurtured.

# 8. Offboarding Protocol and Onboarding Protocol

## 8.1. Onboarding

Objective: Ensure that every new member not only learns processes and tools, but also feels like a citizen of Numinia, integrated at operational, narrative, and cultural levels.

Phases:

### Phase Fase 0 (pre-onboarding real): Marco Legal y Contrato Adaptable

Objective: Ensure that every new member not only learns processes and tools, but also feels like a citizen of Numinia — integrated on operational, narrative, and cultural levels.

Phases:

### Phase 0 (pre–real onboarding): Legal Framework and Adaptable Contract

Objective: reduce uncertainty and generate enthusiasm; make the person feel expected and welcomed.

Gate (rule to enter Phase 1): contract/NDA signed and status plan agreed upon.

Checklist:

- Contract and NDA sent and signed. (Procurator)
- Documentation received (ID, SS/Social Security, IBAN; if freelancer: billing data). (Procurator)
- Added to payroll/HR system or vendor registry (if freelancer). (Procurator)
- Status plan by phases: freelancer → staff (target date + conversion criteria). (Manager + Procurator)

### Phase 1: Welcome & Numinia Citizenship

Objective: get to know the person through the Numen/Numinia lens, map alignment (role ↔ guild/faction), and deliver the complete package (lore+ core + operational) before granting full access.

Gates:

- Key 1:1s conducted → Pack delivered.

Checklist:

- 1:1 script (template) shared: motivations, pace, prior tools, communication preferences, risks/expectations. (Buddy)
- 1:1s conducted and summary in Huly ("Preliminary Working Agreement": schedules, channels, DoR/DoD, boundaries). (Buddy + Person)
- Delivery of digital "Welcome Pack" and acknowledgment of index review. (Procurator)

### Phase 2: First Weeks — Orientation & Foundations

Objective: integrate culture and provide tools to start working autonomously.

**Culture and Vision**

- Session: history, mission/vision, values of Numen/Numinia. (Sentinel/Exegete)
- Structure: guilds/factions, active projects, stakeholder map. (Sentinel + Guild Lead)

**Role and Expectations**

- Define role responsibilities (documented in Huly). (Manager)
- Set initial 30/60/90 objectives (Huly OKRs or equivalent). (Manager)
- Agree on KPIs/success criteria for the role. (Manager + Person)

**Training**

- Training in tools, if needed.
- Key meetings with interacting areas.

**Connection and Follow-up**

- 1:1 circuit (scheduled in Phase 1).
- Weekly closing check-in (feelings, doubts, blockers). (Manager)
- Informal Buddy check-in. (Buddy)
- Voluntary invitation to observe rituals: Dark Council (strategy) and Lunar Coven (creativity). (Sentinel)

### Phase 3: First 30 Days — Operational Integration

Objective: begin assuming responsibilities and building effective working relationships.

**Performance**

- Assign manageable tasks/projects (Mission 0 or equivalent) with clear DoD. (Manager + Guild Lead)
- Encourage initiative and short internal demo (show & tell). (Guild Lead)

**Continuous Learning**

- Continue specific role and product training. (Buddy + Guild Lead)
- Identify training gaps and plan closure. (Manager)

**Feedback**

- Weekly 1:1 follow-up (bidirectional): barriers, needs, support. (Manager)

**Social**

- Ensure inclusion in social dynamics (virtual coffees, themed channels). (Buddy)

### Phase 4: Días 60–90 — Autonomía & Proyección

Objective: consolidate learning, evaluate initial performance, and project development.

**Evaluation**

- Formal review around day 90 (achievements vs. 30/60/90 goals, KPIs). (Manager + Person)
- Celebrate milestones; document strengths and improvement areas. (Manager)

**Development**

- Career plan and growth conversation (guild/faction path). (Manager + Guild Lead)
- Define 6-month objectives and next mission set. (Manager)

**Process Feedback**

- Collect formal onboarding feedback from the person. (Procurator).
- Evaluate whether the Buddy role is reduced or concluded. (Manager + Buddy).
- (If applicable) Freelancer → staff conversion according to agreed plan and criteria.
- (Optional) Narrative recognition: assign citizen title (e.g., Apprentice Alchemist).

**The 4 "C"s of Onboarding (mapped to Numen/Numinia)**

- Compliance: contract/NDAs, policies, security/GDPR, access. (Procurator)
- Clarity: responsibilities, 30/60/90, KPIs, Working Agreement. (Manager)
- Culture: Numinia citizenship, guilds/factions, rituals, values. (Sentinel)
- Connection: team, Buddy, 1:1 circuit, social spaces. (Manager + Buddy)

## 8.2. Offboarding

Objective: close the relationship while caring for both the person and the organization: knowledge transfer, security, legal/GDPR compliance, narrative closure within Numinia (citizenship, guild/faction), and maintenance of the alumni bond.

Roles: Procurator (Ops/HR), Manager, Buddy, Sentinel, Guild Lead, IT, Finance.

Evidence: each item with link/attachment in Huly (minutes, forms, access screenshots, etc.).

### Fase 0: Trigger & Exit Plan (pre–real offboarding)

Objective: agree on conditions, dates, and exit plan before touching systems.

Gate (to Phase 1): confirmed last working day (LWD) + exit type + approved plan.

Checklist:

- Confirm exit type: voluntary / end of service (freelancer) / termination. (Procurator + Manager)
- Define LWD and handover window (e.g., 10–15 working days). (Manager)
- Review contract/ANNEXES: notice, clauses, pending materials, IP. (Procurator)
- Agree on final package: settlement/payroll, vacation, bonus/prorated items; for freelancers: final invoice + payment schedule. (Procurator + Finance)
- Draft and publish Offboarding Plan in Huly (who does what and when). (Procurator)

### Fase 1: Communication & Civic Ritual (human announcement before technical)

Objective: communicate respectfully and clearly; activate narrative closure in Numinia.

Gate: internal announcement made + person informed of their plan.

Checklist:

- 1:1 conversation Manager ↔ person (message, reasons, next steps). (Manager)
- Internal announcement (team + stakeholders): respectful tone, LWD, handover contact. (Manager)
- Narrative closure ritual: farewell in open channel (optional), acknowledgment and citizenship closure (e.g., "Citizen Emeritus" / Alumni). (Sentinel)
- Register whether the person wishes to join Numinia Alumni (channel/newsletter). (Procurator + Sentinel)

### Fase 2: Knowledge & Handover (operational transfer)

Objective: protect continuity: extract tacit knowledge, document, and reassign.

Gate: handover approved by Manager + Guild Lead.

Checklist:

- List open missions/artifacts in Huly (status, risks, next steps). (Person + Manager)
- Create Handover Doc (template):
  - Context and current goals
  - Pending decisions
  - Stakeholders and rhythms
  - Critical links (repos, dashboards, credentials in vault)
  - "First 14 days without me" operational checklist (Person)
- Demonstration/Walkthrough for successor (short recording). (Person + Buddy)
- Reassign ownership in Huly/GitHub/AWS/Hyperfy/M365 (owners, reviewers, alerts). (Manager + IT)
- Inventory of secrets (tokens/APIs/keys) and post-exit rotation plan. (IT)

### Phase 3: Security & Access (staggered technical execution)

Objective: revoke access securely and orderly without hindering handover.

Gate: security checklist completed + IT verification.

Checklist:

- Selective freeze: limit high-risk permissions (prod, billing) while keeping handover access. (IT)
- Export relevant mail/calendar to team (per policy); configure OOO/auto-reply until LWD. (IT)
- Transfer ownership of repositories / boards / dashboards. (IT + Manager)
- Revoke access at end of LWD: SSO/MFA, Discord, Huly, GitHub, Hyperfy, AWS, M365, VPN. (IT)
- Rotate secrets (tokens, SSH, API keys, AWS IAM). (IT)
- Validate "no orphan assets": no unowned resources (repos, buckets, licenses). (IT)

### Phase 4: Logistics & Legal (equipment, IP, personal data)

Objective: close material and legal aspects per GDPR/contracts.

Gate: signed handover/receipt + IP closure.

Checklist:

- Return of equipment (or purchase confirmation if policy allows): laptop, peripherals, physical keys. (Procurator + Person)
- Device wipe/cleaning (if applicable) and proof. (IT)
- End of GDPR processing: revoke access to personal data; apply retention/deletion policy. (Procurator + IT)
- Final IP transfer: ensure all deliverables are in official repos; sign if contractually required. (Procurator)
- For freelancers: final invoice + deliverable validation; for staff: settlement and legal exit docs. (Finance + Procurator)

### Phase 5: Human Closure & Exit Survey

Objective: learn from the process, end the relationship well, and leave doors open.

Gate: exit survey registered + final feedback shared.

Checklist:

- Exit Interview (30–45 min) with script: reasons, what worked/didn't, suggestions, culture, leadership. (Procurator)
- Final Manager feedback: recognition and team learnings. (Manager)
- Offer reference (if applicable) and Alumni channel. (Manager + Procurator)
- Exit survey (short anonymous form) + quarterly summary. (Procurator)

### Phase 6: Post-Exit (D+7 a D+30)

Objective: ensure nothing is left pending and complete accounting/security.

Gate: internal "clean slate" audit passed.

Checklist:

- Audit for residual access (shadow IT, OAuth apps). (IT)
- Confirm secret rotation completed successfully. (IT)
- Financial closure: pending payments (salaries, vacation, bonus; or freelancer invoices). (Finance)
- Archive artifacts and relink documentation to new owners. (Manager)
- If requested: deliver personal data package (portability) per GDPR. (Procurator)

**Mapa 4 "C" aplicado al Offboarding**

- Compliance: Phases 0, 3, 4, 6 (contract, IP, GDPR, access, payments).
- Clarity: Phase 0 (plan), Phase 2 (Handover Doc), Phase 3 (security checklist), Phase 6 (audit).
- Culture: Phase 1 (closure ritual, thanks), Alumni.
- Connection: Phase 1 (respectful communication), Phase 2 (peer handover), Phase 5 (exit interview)

**Definition of Done — Offboarding (resumen)**

- Offboarding plan approved with confirmed LWD.
- Communication completed + closure ritual (if desired).
- Handover documented, ownership reassigned, and demo recorded.
- Access revoked and secrets rotated; no "orphan assets."
- Logistics and legal closure done (equipment, IP, GDPR, settlement/invoice).
- Exit interview + survey registered; clean D+30 audit passed.

# 10. Scrum — Definition of Done (DoD)

The Definition of Done (DoD) is crucial in Scrum because it establishes a shared understanding within the Scrum Team of what it means for work to be "done." Without a clear DoD, different team members might have different interpretations of when a Product Backlog item is complete, which can lead to issues such as:

- Lack of transparency: It's not clear what has actually been completed.
- Accumulation of "half-done work": Items considered "almost ready" but that don't meet all the necessary criteria to be deployable or usable.
- Quality problems: Without clear criteria regarding testing, documentation, etc., the quality of the Increment could be compromised.

## What does a Definition of Done typically include?

The DoD varies from one team to another and even between different products. However, some common elements often included are:

- Complete coding: All necessary code for the functionality has been written.
- Unit tests passed: All unit tests succeed.
- Integration tests completed: Tests verifying interactions with other components work properly.
- System tests performed: Tests that evaluate the complete system functionality.
- Code review completed: Another team member has reviewed the code.
- Documentation updated: All relevant documentation (for users, developers, etc.) is up to date.
- Integrated with previous Increment: The new work has been properly integrated with previously completed parts of the product.
- Deployable: The Increment is in a state where it could be delivered to end users (even if it's not deployed every Sprint).
- Acceptance criteria met: For each Product Backlog item, the defined acceptance criteria have been fulfilled.

## Who defines the Definition of Done?

If the organization has established standards, the team's Definition of Done must at least comply with them. The Development Team defines the DoD, often in collaboration with the Scrum Master and the Product Owner. It is essential that the entire Scrum Team agrees on and understands the DoD.
