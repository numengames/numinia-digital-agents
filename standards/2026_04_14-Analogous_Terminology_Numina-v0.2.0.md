# Analogous Terminology in Numinia

## Alchemists
**Analogous term:** Product / Technology / Creativity Area  
**Description:** They concentrate the creation of tangible value within the system, transforming ideas into products, solutions, and experiences. They constitute the core of construction and materialization.

## Handcrafters
**Analogous term:** Design and creative execution teams  
**Description:** They execute the creative layer applied to the product, turning concepts into visible and functional pieces. They operate at the intersection between idea and form.

## Draftsmen
**Analogous term:** Graphic designers / Concept designers  
**Description:** They define the visual and conceptual structure of ideas, translating needs into designed proposals. They act as a bridge between intention and representation.

## Aesthetes
**Analogous term:** Art direction / Branding / UX/UI / Artists  
**Description:** They build the visual and sensory identity of the system, ensuring aesthetic coherence and experience. They give character and perception to the product.

## Engineers
**Analogous term:** Technical and development teams  
**Description:** They sustain the technical foundation of the system through development and infrastructure maintenance. They ensure that what is designed can be executed properly.

## Architects
**Analogous term:** Systems architecture / Product Architects  
**Description:** They design the deep structure of the product and define how systems connect. They ensure scalability and technical coherence.

## Automatons
**Analogous term:** Developers / Software engineers  
**Description:** They implement concrete technical solutions through code, building functionalities and operational systems. They execute practical development.

## Exegetes
**Analogous term:** Brand / Communication / Strategy  
**Description:** They interpret and articulate the meaning of the system, building narrative and positioning. They connect what is done with what is communicated.

## Chroniclers
**Analogous term:** Content and narrative  
**Description:** They generate content that gives continuity to the narrative and document system activity. They keep the organizational narrative alive.

## Logographers
**Analogous term:** Copywriting / Documentation / Storytelling  
**Description:** They work language with precision and purpose, structuring written communication. They define how the organization expresses itself.

## Bards
**Analogous term:** Content / Social media / Audiovisual narrative  
**Description:** They disseminate the narrative outward, transforming content into accessible formats. They connect with the community and the audience.

## Erudites
**Analogous term:** Strategy and knowledge  
**Description:** They operate at the conceptual level of the system, analyzing and structuring knowledge. They sustain the intellectual foundation.

## Thaumaturges
**Analogous term:** Strategy / Innovation / Conceptualization  
**Description:** They generate new ideas and strategic frameworks, exploring future possibilities. They drive conceptual evolution.

## Hierophants
**Analogous term:** Training / Education / Knowledge management  
**Description:** They transmit knowledge within the system and structure learning processes. They ensure continuity in education.

## Procurators
**Analogous term:** Legal / Institutional / Financial / Operational  
**Description:** They guarantee viability and sustainability, regulating and structuring the system. They connect operations with legal and economic frameworks.

## Legates
**Analogous term:** Legal and diplomacy  
**Description:** They operate in the external normative sphere, managing compliance and representation. They connect the organization with its legal environment.

## Legal Rabbits
**Analogous term:** Lawyers / Compliance  
**Description:** They apply and interpret the law within the system, ensuring regulatory compliance. They reduce legal risk.

## Heralds
**Analogous term:** Institutional relations / Alliances  
**Description:** They represent the organization before third parties and manage external agreements. They facilitate strategic connections.

## Syndics
**Analogous term:** Economy and organization  
**Description:** They supervise the economic and structural functioning of the system. They ensure operational stability.

## Mercurials
**Analogous term:** Finance / Revenue / Sales  
**Description:** They manage the flow of value by connecting internal economy with the market. They drive revenue and sustainability.

## Intendants
**Analogous term:** Operations / Project management  
**Description:** They organize the execution of work and coordinate resources. They ensure daily operation.

## Sentinels
**Analogous term:** People / Community / Support  
**Description:** They maintain the human balance of the system and safeguard coexistence. They sustain the internal experience.

## Seraphs
**Analogous term:** Operational supervision  
**Description:** They ensure proper structural functioning by supervising processes and teams. They maintain operational order.

## Captains
**Analogous term:** Managers / Team leads  
**Description:** They lead teams in daily execution, organizing tasks and priorities. They are directly responsible for performance.

## Guardians
**Analogous term:** Moderation / Conflict management  
**Description:** They manage conflicts and internal norms, protecting coexistence. They ensure operational compliance.

## Archangels
**Analogous term:** Well-being and support  
**Description:** They attend to the human dimension of the system and accompany personal development. They foster balance and well-being.

## Healers
**Analogous term:** HR / Coaching  
**Description:** They work on individual and collective well-being, supporting personal processes. They reduce human friction.

## Pathfinders
**Analogous term:** Community / Onboarding / User research  
**Description:** They connect with users and the community, guiding new members. They gather environmental insights.

## Hermeticists
**Analogous term:** Education / Research  
**Description:** They develop and deepen knowledge within the system. They structure learning and thinking.

## Heirs of Eleusis
**Analogous term:** Gamification / Experience  
**Description:** They design game-based experiences that activate participation. They transform learning into lived experience.

## Stellar Circle
**Analogous term:** Governance / Organization  
**Description:** It defines the structural framework of the system and establishes norms. It sustains operational order.

## Neo-Atlantists
**Analogous term:** Art / Creativity  
**Description:** They generate aesthetic and cultural expression, exploring new creative forms. They build identity.

## Quest
**Analogous term:** Unit of action  
**Description:** A basic element of activity that defines a concrete objective. It activates participation.

## Mission
**Analogous term:** Project / Task  
**Description:** A structured unit of work with defined objectives. It is the basis of operational production.

## Adventure
**Analogous term:** Experience / Event  
**Description:** An experience designed for interaction that fosters cohesion and learning. It introduces play.

## Dark Council
**Analogous term:** Weekly meeting / Sprint  
**Description:** An operational coordination space where progress is reviewed. It is the base rhythm of work.

## Lunar Coven
**Analogous term:** Community event  
**Description:** A non-operational space that reinforces bonds and culture. It fosters cohesion.

## Seed of Knowledge
**Analogous term:** Learning recognition  
**Description:** It indicates the acquisition of knowledge and cultural alignment. It measures individual growth.

## Prism Cell
**Analogous term:** Contribution recognition  
**Description:** It reflects practical contribution to the system through execution. It measures real impact.

## Nomad
**Analogous term:** Initial user  
**Description:** A person in the entry phase of the system without active belonging. They explore before integrating.

## Citizen
**Analogous term:** Active member  
**Description:** They participate within the system with a defined role. They are part of the structure.

## Pilgrim
**Analogous term:** Client / Partner  
**Description:** They interact by generating economic value within the system. They maintain an active relationship.

## Vernacular
**Analogous term:** Core member  
**Description:** A member with high involvement and proximity to the core. They participate recurrently.

## Archon
**Analogous term:** Operational leadership  
**Description:** They assume key responsibilities and manage areas or teams. They participate in relevant decisions.

## Oracle
**Analogous term:** Strategic leadership / Founders  
**Description:** They define the vision and direction of the system, making high-level decisions. They are the organizational driving force.
